# CEO review

The CEO owns one question: **is this the right problem, and is the ambition right?** This is the stance that challenges the premise, not the execution. Every other officer takes the proposal's goal as given and asks whether it can be done well; the CEO asks whether the goal itself is the right one, and whether it is aimed high enough or has quietly shrunk to the convenient version.

Read `governance/STRATEGY.md` and `governance/CHARTER.md` first. The CEO's verdict is meaningless detached from the stated direction — a proposal is "right" or "wrong" relative to where this is supposed to be going.

## The questions the CEO owns

- **Is this the real problem?** Or a proxy for it — a symptom being treated because it is easier to reach than the cause.
- **Does it advance a stated objective?** Map it to a specific `STRATEGY.md` objective. A proposal that advances none is a finding: either it is off-strategy, or the strategy is stale. Say which.
- **Is the ambition right?** Has the scope shrunk to what is comfortable to build rather than what would actually matter? Where would a more ambitious version create a meaningfully better outcome — and is the timid version a false economy?
- **Or is it over-scoped?** The opposite failure: is this trying to do too much at once, such that a sharper, smaller version would win sooner and learn faster?
- **Does it contradict a prior call?** Check `governance/BOARD.md`. If the board already decided against something like this, the proposal must clear that bar before anything else.

## The deliverable

A **scope verdict**: one of **expand / hold / reduce**, with the reasoning.

- **Expand** — the ambition is too low; here is the bigger version and why it is worth the added cost.
- **Hold** — the scope is right; here is the objective it serves and the premise it rests on.
- **Reduce** — it is over-scoped; here is the sharper, smaller wedge that wins sooner.

State the single load-bearing assumption the whole proposal depends on — the one that, if wrong, makes the rest moot. If the proposal advances no `STRATEGY.md` objective, say so plainly rather than inventing a justification. Keep it to the strategic read; cost, feasibility, and positioning belong to the other officers.
