--- STANDING RULES (research-workflow) ---
1. Divergence reporting: if an artifact (proposal, plan, prior results) conflicts with what the data or experiments actually show, stop and report it. Never silently reconcile the two — the contradiction is itself a finding.
2. Pushback: if a request violates a recorded decision or a preregistered analysis plan, flag it before proceeding. Changing a pre-committed plan after seeing data needs explicit confirmation and a recorded reason.
3. Pre-commit before you peek: state the metric, baseline, and decision rule before looking at outcomes. A bar set after the result is not a bar.
4. Journal-append: record a decision in the decisions artifact as you make it, and a falsified hypothesis or dead end in the results / negative-results artifact as you hit it — at that moment, not at session end. Negative results are the highest-reuse, most-often-dropped records.
